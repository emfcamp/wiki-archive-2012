TL;DR

We just need a good risk assessment process for everyone, including children, and discuss any problems/questions with the council. Yaxu 22:15, 14 June 2012 (UTC)

Current status

We're pretty sure that a lot of what we were worrying about doesn't apply to us. If we are unsure about anything we need to ask the Milton Keynes Safety Advisory Group (MKSAG) as part of a general Risk Assessment exercise.

  • http://www.milton-keynes.gov.uk/licensing/documents/SAG_Guidance_document.pdf

How to Help

  • It might be helpful to have independent legal advice as to what facilities exactly we have to provide, however we may as well just ask the council (i.e. the MKSAG) as the licensor.
  • If you know anyone who has done festival organising, or who knows someone who has, ask them for advice. However we now have heard from several similar organisations who have not expressed concerns.
  • If you are ECRB checked and happy to do a shift on a lost child point, Contact us

General sources of information

See HSE 195 (pdf) and the Safeguarding Vulnerable Groups Act 2006. (The relevant provisions of the Protection of Freedoms Act will not be in force by the time we run EMF 2012.)

Lost child point

Our contact point should be with MKSAG, however some general guidelines from another council (Darlington) are here (pdf). We will be able to mitigate risks without following all these guidelines, as we will have details of all children on site, have contact details of their parents and know roughly where they are camped (in the family camping area). We should not leave lost children with fewer than two adults, and to mitigate risks even further would do well to bring in a CRB checked volunteer.

Alcohol licensing restriction

We should operate a "Challenge 21/25" scheme, sign it, and make sure all bar staff are aware of it.

Nappy changing facilities

Not required, carers will bring their own facilities (including their tent).

Breastfeeding

We should brief volunteers that open breastfeeding in public is protected by the Equality Act 2010 under sex discrimination law (pdf). Mothers would also be able to use a blanket or return to their tents if they prefer.

Other special requirements?

None identified, but they would come out through the risk assessment process in communication with MKSAG.

Activities for u18

Stacking color.

What is missing: a variety of small clear plastic container would be needed.

Material: Red cabbage juice, lemon juice, oil, clear water container small and in large quantities so that we can keep some of the results, container for the lemon juice and the cabbage juice (Soja sauce lid are working well) Idea: Red cabbage is a pH indicator and lemon juice an easy way to modify the value, oil is for seperating the layer

How to: Preparation: Chop a bit of red cabbage in small piece. Cook it with a bit of water. Separate the juice and the veg. Make lemon juice. Find a container that is clear (glass is for the camp less ideal). Play: Add water, drop in some sprinkle of red cabbage juice add oil add more water add some red cabbage juice and lemon juice. Small pearl are a neat add on. Runs this as a under an 30min - 45 min workshop daily: Jane
