Portcullis Projectile Potato Mash Off

From Electromagnetic Field

A few of us at Portcullis fancied making something cool at the camp, and mulled over various ideas, until we decided that we should run a potato cannon competition. Anyone can enter, and the rules are fairly minimal.

Rules

General

  • Only one cannon per team.
  • Any number of members allowed for a team.
  • Cannon must have been manufactured during the camp.
  • Cannon may have any dimensions, and be made of any material, however entries are subject to scrutineering prior to competition, and the judge's decision is final.
  • Any size and type of potato may be used as ammunition. Teams may provide their own ammunition, if not ammunition will be provided by the judges. Potatoes must be solid, and 100% potato.

Important

  • Any entries must not produce a muzzle energy of more than 12ft/lbs, otherwise they are classed as a firearm under UK law.
  • How ever you fire your potato it must not do damage to the park (no scorch marks or furrows in the turf, please!)

Combustion Based

  • Maximum 15psi in the combustion chamber prior to ignition
  • No acetylene, solid or liquid fuels (if in doubt, please ask a judge)

Pneumatic Entries

  • Maximum 150psi in the chamber prior to firing

Firing

  • No individual may be closer than 3 metres to the entry once the potato has been loaded. Potatoes must be loaded before any fuel is added to the chamber or pressurisation begins.

The Competition

Entries will be split into two categories: Combustion, and Pneumatic. Quite simply, if you ignite something, it's combustion based, otherwise it's probably pneumatic.

Round One: Distance

  • Three shots to launch your ammunition as far as you can.

First Place (furthest potato): 3 points Second Place (second furthest potato): 2 points Third Place (third furthest potato): 1 point

Round Two: Accuracy

  • Three shots to land your ammnunition as close to the target as possible.

First Place (closest potato): 3 points Second Place (second closest potato): 2 points Third Place (third closest potato): 1 point
