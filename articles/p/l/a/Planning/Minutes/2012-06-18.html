Power (Russ)

Issues from previous meeting

  • 13A/16A plug will fit into portaloo under the door, should not be an issue.. Anything > 32A will live outside but that is all IP44 or IP67 anyway.
  • Cables as supplied from hire companies are usually H07RN. Items of kit like lights are sometimes supplied with blue arctic. I don't consider this an issue.
  • Re breaker size v- 6A/16A - Will and Beeny are still looking into this. Issues with breaker discrimination in the distribution design. Don't see occasional high current devices like kettles as an problem. We should provide a mechanism for users to request higher power if they need it.
  • Gauging station - decision made to not pursue at this stage, power not really in a useful location. We would like to use the standalone 20KVA singlephase though.

New progress/issues:

  • Benny now being assisted by Will-h and Natm. There seems to be lots of crossover.
  • At the stage of needing an accurate site plan to scale distances and plan.
  • Benny has secured some GRP portable building units. Flatpack. Ideal for power/network storage.
  • Not yet at budget stage, but this is progressing well. talked to 3+ suppliers.
  • Have planned for a central 'services' area to the north of the helipad adjacent to the track comprising generators, distribution, mast. Will need to be fenced. Views welcome.
  • Campervan field - if this is the other side of the bridge to the west of the site, the power run is 300M. This is likely unworkeable from a power POV. If it ends up here will probably have to hire additional generator.
  • Logistics/Telehandler/Bridge: Low bridge is getting to be a major issue. Usual on a festival site for things to be delivered in bins/on skids, all the power stuff would normally be delivered on 19ton flatbed with hiab, which will not fit under the bridge. This is likely to be true for toilets and many other items too. We may need a telehandler/skidsteer to move things around onsite. Will is looking into it.
  • Bridge - Beeny and Will are considering pumping out under the bridge so we can see whats there + assess likely passability for vehicles.
  • Generators - 135kva > units are supplied by lorry and offloaded using a crane, made enquiries into using a telehandler to move under the bridge, but units will weigh too much. Current avenue is 2 x 100kva towable sync'd units, Nat is getting prices.

Sanitation (Crypt)

  • We need a confirmed final Datenklo count (currently 18)
  • 20% deposit for toilets is needed at the beginning of August
  • Fencing is still not tied down: may be reduced in cost
  • Waiting for a response from Anglian Water on supply. The Barrel Bikers said there's a stand-pipe on site, but that's unknown to MKPT.
  • Rubbish will need access for a large vehicle. The Barrel Bikers say it's fine for large vehicles to use the water dip under the bridge, as it has a hard-standing base and they've got skip vehicles through before.

Internet (Jasper)

Issues from previous meeting

  • Confident with regards to placing the tower at the main site and the height it can achieve, Pulsant are ok with a temporary mast in their car park too.
  • Backup: FTTP - logistics regarding fibre runs and temporary power + housing for CPE are not practical. Would prove problematic with regards to failover and routing of the CCC address space, plus having to deal with neighbours.
  • Backup: PtP - investigating a 2nd wireless PtP link using Ubiquiti Rockets or loan kit from Cisco.

New progress/issues:

  • Router installed at Pulsant MK, OOB and L2 to be connected this week.
  • Optics loan progressing with FlexiOptix, awaiting approval from their HQ. (10x1000T + 10x1000SX in June, 30x1000T in August).
  • Towers are being booked up for summer events, need to pay 50% (720 inc VAT) ASAP to secure 2 of them.
  • Require accurate site plan to allow us to progress with design and planning, before final kit collection from Cisco.
  • Jasper's off from this week, so someone else will need to provide updates
